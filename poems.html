My Poems

While the Stars Remain

Twenty-three years I’ve been here A blink, a breath And the stars have not changed They wait, indifferent Scattered across the same vast sky As if time were something that only belonged To me In ten thousand years, they will still shine Unmoved by the lives that rise and fall Beneath them Some will burn out, fading into silence Some will explode, sending echoes through the universe Their light chasing a future that may never arrive I will be long gone before they change My years vanishing like a ship in the night And maybe in ten thousand years, There will be no eyes left to look up No one left to call them stars Humanity—a brief streak of motion There, then not While the stars remain

My Stellar Birthplace

When I was a little girl, I used to imagine myself flying through a nebula— floating through the cosmos with the elements that made mankind. I’d close my eyes and drift. Colors would rise around me— lavender, rust, rose-gold, folding like wings, or arms. I didn’t need to steer. The stars knew where I was going. Dust spun slow as thought, and light touched my skin like it remembered me. There was no noise— only warmth, and the steady rhythm of being part of something vast and very old. I never called it love. But it was close. And now, every time a new image appears— bright and distant on a screen or in my hands— something inside me lifts: quiet, stardust-small, and weightless once more.

A Flicker of You

You were the northern lights-- Rare, Silent, And impossible to forget Not always there, But when you were, The world stood still Sky cracked open in color And for a moment, Everything felt Brighter I didn’t always know how to name you-- Just that your laugh Lingered like the shimmer, And your stories Danced slow through the dark Now you’re just out of reach Just past the edge of where I can follow But sometimes When the night is quiet Andd the air hums with memory, I feel you again-- A flicker in my chest, A warmth that doesn’t fade The northern lights don’t last forever But once you’ve seen them, They're always yours

Eulogy in Stardust

I find peace in knowing That when I die My body will be transformed The first law of Thermodynamics states, “Energy cannot be created or destroyed, Only converted from one form to another” When I die That stuff that I’m made of Will be transformed But never forgotten Never lost to the whims of the universe You will find me in everything The grass The trees The breeze you feel On a cold winter morning And when the Sun inevitably Swallows the Earth whole, My body will be made Of everything that has been Or ever will be I find peace in knowing That when I die My body will be transformed
