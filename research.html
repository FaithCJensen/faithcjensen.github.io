Research

Under the supervision of Dr. Chantanelle Nava, I research how stellar activity biases low-mass planet parameters. We utilized HARPS-N solar data to perform injection and recovery simulations to quantify the impact of seasonal stellar variability on the recovery of low-mass planet parameter estimates.

Current Project

Injected-Planet Recovery in Seasonal HARPS-N Solar Data

I analyze three years of HARPS-N solar radial-velocity observations, divided into seasonal datasets that reflect realistic observing gaps. Into each season, I inject a synthetic planet signal (K = 1.0 m/s, P = 80.0 days, e = 0) to evaluate how well different fitting strategies recover the injected planet parameters.

Role: generated RV injections, implemented quasi-periodic Gaussian process (QPGP) models to fit RV data, performed MCMC fitting, and compared parameter recovery across seasons.

This work assesses how modeling the seasonal affects of stellar activity can improve low-mass exoplanet parameter estimates. A manuscript is currently in prep.
